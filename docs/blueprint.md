# **App Name**: Retro Manager

## Core Features:

- Game Setup & Persistence: Start a new game, load a saved game, allow selection of an initial team, and enable saving of game progress.
- Squad & Tactics Overview: Display player attributes, current fitness, and morale. Allow managers to adjust team formation and basic tactical instructions for upcoming matches.
- Basic Transfer Market: Enable searching for available players, submitting transfer offers for desired players, and managing incoming transfer offers from other clubs.
- Text-Based Match Engine: Simulate football matches with minute-by-minute text commentary, generating scores, player ratings, basic match statistics, and key events like goals, cards, and injuries.
- League & Season Progression: Maintain and display league tables, a fixture list, and handle the weekly game cycle and end-of-season transitions, including promotions and relegations.
- In-Game News & Messages: Provide critical updates through a messaging system, including player injuries, transfer rumors, board feedback, and pre/post-match summaries.
- Simple Financials: Present the club's basic financial status, including a budget overview and a summary of weekly player wages.

## Style Guidelines:

- The visual design will use a muted, dark color scheme, inspired by early computer interfaces. The primary interactive color will be a desaturated blue (#6699CC), reminiscent of classic monitor displays, providing a clean yet distinctive focal point. The background will be a dark, heavily desaturated charcoal blue (#20262C), ensuring high contrast for text readability in a retro aesthetic. An accent color of bright cyan (#26D9D9) will highlight crucial information and interactive elements, providing a modern touch while respecting the retro sensibility.
- All text, from headlines to body content, will utilize 'Inter', a grotesque-style sans-serif. Its modern, machined, and neutral appearance perfectly suits the data-dense, functional, and efficient nature of the application, aligning with a retro computer UI feel without resorting to pixelation.
- Iconography will be designed with a minimalistic, 1-bit or pixel-art style, consistent with the game's retro aesthetic. Simple, blocky graphical elements will convey actions and information clearly without relying on modern embellishments.
- The layout will emphasize clarity and efficiency, employing a predominantly list-based and modular grid system for presenting data. Similar to a spreadsheet, content will be organized within clean, functional 'window' elements, prioritizing information density and rapid navigation over excessive whitespace.
- Animations will be intentionally sparse, mimicking the limitations of early computing. Where transitions are necessary, they will be quick and 'chunky,' evocative of the screen updates on vintage systems like Amiga or DOS, rather than fluid or continuous movements.
